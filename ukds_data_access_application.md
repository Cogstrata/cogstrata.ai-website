## 1. About CogStrata

CogStrata is a demographic intelligence platform that transforms open and licensed data into enriched, postcode-level profiles for use by enterprise teams and AI-driven applications. Our platform aggregates data across geography, census demographics, deprivation indices, income, housing, transport, and economic indicators to produce derived, area-level statistical attributes. We do not hold, process, or derive any individual-level personal data — all outputs are aggregate and area-based.

Our UK data product currently draws on a range of open government sources including the ONS Postcode Directory, Census 2021, the Index of Multiple Deprivation, and other publicly available datasets published under the Open Government Licence. We are now seeking to incorporate additional data collections held by the UK Data Service to deepen the analytical value of our platform.

## 3. Project Information

**Project title:** CogStrata Demographic Intelligence Platform — Research & Development Phase

**Project summary:**
CogStrata is building an area-level demographic intelligence platform that enriches UK postcodes with derived statistical attributes spanning housing tenure, educational attainment, household composition, deprivation, income estimation, and segmentation. The requested data collections will be used during an initial research and development phase to validate, calibrate, and improve the accuracy of our proprietary inference models. During this phase, the raw source data will not be redistributed, resold, or made available to any third party. Only derived, aggregate-level statistical outputs — which are not individually identifiable and cannot be reverse-engineered to reconstruct the original data — will be incorporated into CogStrata's products.

**Research objectives:**

- Validate and benchmark CogStrata's proprietary demographic inference models against authoritative survey and census microdata held by the UK Data Service.
- Improve the accuracy of area-level derived attributes (e.g. household income estimation, financial stress indicators, educational attainment distributions) by calibrating model parameters against the requested datasets.
- Explore the feasibility of deriving new area-level statistical indicators from the source data that would add analytical value to CogStrata's platform.

**Methodology:**
The requested data will be processed in a secure, access-controlled environment. Analysis will involve statistical aggregation, model calibration, and validation at area level (Output Area, LSOA, or postcode). No individual-level data will be published, shared, or incorporated into any external product. All outputs will be aggregate statistical derivatives at area level.

## 4. Compliance with the End User Licence Agreement

CogStrata confirms its commitment to full compliance with the UK Data Service End User Licence Agreement, including:

- **Non-commercial use (initial phase):** The requested data will be used exclusively for non-commercial research and development purposes during the initial access period. No revenue will be generated directly or indirectly from the source data during this phase.
- **No redistribution of source data:** Raw data collections will not be shared, distributed, sold, or made available to any third party, whether in whole, in part, or in modified form.
- **No individual identification:** CogStrata will not attempt to identify, re-identify, or derive information relating to any individual or household from the data collections.
- **Data security:** Access credentials will be stored securely and not shared. Data will be held in an access-controlled environment in accordance with the UK Data Service's Research Data Handling and Security guidance.
- **Statistical disclosure control:** Any published outputs will comply with the disclosure control standards specified by the UK Data Service.
- **Acknowledgement and citation:** All publications, reports, or documentation referencing the data will include full acknowledgement of the original data creators, depositors, and the UK Data Service in the prescribed format.
- **Data destruction:** All copies of the source data (including backups, subsets, and temporary files) will be destroyed at the end of the access period in accordance with the EUL requirements.
- **Online data tools:** CogStrata will not use any online or AI-based data tools in connection with the source data unless explicit written permission is obtained from the Data Service Provider.

## 5. Request for Commercial Licensing Discussion

CogStrata acknowledges that the EUL Agreement restricts use to non-commercial purposes, and that commercial use requires prior permission from the Registering Organisation and/or the relevant Data Service Provider (per Clause 4 of the EUL).

We are requesting that, alongside granting non-commercial access, the UK Data Service consider the following:

**a) Confirmation that our intended non-commercial R&D use is permitted:**
Specifically, that using the data to calibrate and validate proprietary statistical models — where only aggregate, area-level derived outputs (not the source data itself) would eventually be incorporated into a commercial product — falls within the scope of permitted non-commercial research during the initial access period.

**b) A pathway to commercial licensing:**
Should our research demonstrate sufficient value, we would like to explore a commercial licence or redistribution agreement that would permit CogStrata to incorporate area-level derived outputs — calibrated or validated using the source data — into our commercial platform. We understand this would require a separate agreement and potentially involve the original data creators and depositors.

**c) Clarification on derived data:**
We would welcome guidance on whether aggregate, area-level statistical derivatives (e.g. model coefficients calibrated against the source data, or area-level indices derived from it) constitute "datasets derived from the data collection" under Clause 4 of the EUL, and if so, what terms would govern their use in a commercial context.

## 6. Proposed Licensing Pathway

We propose the following phased approach:

| Phase | Duration | Activity | Licensing |
|-------|----------|----------|-----------|
| **Phase 1 — R&D** | 12 months | Model validation, calibration, and feasibility testing | Standard EUL (non-commercial) |
| **Phase 2 — Review** | At Phase 1 conclusion | Present findings to UK Data Service; discuss derived outputs | Transition discussion |

This phased approach ensures that CogStrata operates within the terms of the EUL at all times, while providing a clear and transparent route to commercial use subject to the agreement of the UK Data Service and relevant data owners.
